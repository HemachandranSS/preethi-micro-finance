🎯 What is APR?

APR stands for Annual Percentage Rate - it's the complete annual cost of borrowing money, expressed as a percentage. Unlike simple interest rates, APR includes all costs associated with your loan.

🔍 The APR Formula

APR = (Total Interest + All Fees) ÷ Loan Amount × 100

This simplified formula works for basic loans. Complex loans may use more sophisticated calculations.

💡 Pro Tips for Smart Borrowing

🔍 Always Compare APRs: When shopping for loans, compare APRs rather than just interest rates to see the true cost.
⚠️ Watch for Variable APRs: Credit cards often have variable APRs that can increase over time.
🕐 Consider Loan Term: APR assumes you'll hold the loan for a full year. Early repayment changes the actual cost.
🔎 Read the Fine Print: Look for hidden fees that might not be included in the advertised APR.
📊 Use APR for Apples-to-Apples Comparison: Different lenders structure fees differently, but APR normalizes the comparison.
